# Aegis Vault × 0G Chain — Meeting Brief (Narrative)

Aegis Vault is a non-custodial, AI-managed, policy-bounded DeFi vault, built 0G-native. The idea in one sentence: the AI proposes a trade, the smart contract decides whether its *shape* is allowed, and operators compete for depositors while putting up stake that can be slashed — and through all of it, the depositor never gives up custody of their funds. Where we stand is just as easy to state plainly: the engineering is already live on 0G mainnet — with the core AI-to-policy-to-DEX pipeline demonstrated on-chain at demo scale — but we are pre-revenue and pre-external-traction, and we are clear-eyed about exactly what it will take to cross over to real capital. That crossing is precisely where 0G can help most.

Before the detail, here is the ask up front, so the conversation has a spine. Our single biggest unlock is deeper liquidity on 0G — as explained below, today's 0G DEX depth caps a functioning vault at only around $1,800 of NAV. Beyond liquidity, we need a small number of warm introductions: one or two credible external AI/quant operators and one risk-curator or allocator. We need help with a first independent security audit, and a milestone-tied grant whose milestones are "deploy V4, run one guarded vault, and publish a real on-chain net-PnL figure." And we would value 0G Compute and Storage support — a model SLA and compute credits for alpha measurement, plus guidance on moving "verifiable inference" from a single signing key toward genuine on-chain enclave-quote verification. On the question of 0G's deep incubation or acceleration program, the short answer is yes: we are actively interested, because the program maps almost one-to-one onto the gaps below.

## 1 · Team and Current Status

On the team itself, this section should be filled in truthfully before the meeting rather than shipped with placeholders. It should name each member with their role and a one-line background — prior DeFi, security, ML, or quant experience and what they have shipped — state the team's size, whether members are full-time, and the working timezone, and note that the project was built for and submitted to the 0G hackathon, making it 0G-native from day one.

What is genuinely built and live deserves to be concrete, because it is verifiable on-chain. The V4 vault stack is deployed on 0G Aristotle mainnet (chain 16661), with the factory at `0x9e36520650Fd7d06CA77Fb0045456c03d3582A5F`. It is a non-custodial, single-owner clone design: a depositor puts in stablecoins, chooses an AI operator, and the vault executes autonomously inside a narrow on-chain policy. Crucially, the AI only *proposes* an EIP-712 intent; the vault itself *enforces* position size, a slippage floor, an asset whitelist, fee caps, a cooldown, intent expiry, and — new in V4 — the operator's committed strategy-manifest hash. Around that vault sits a complete operator-marketplace contract stack, wired to a governor and treasury from t-zero: an operator registry, slashable-USDC staking (up to 50% over a seven-day window), on-chain reputation, an insurance pool (deployed but not yet capitalized), a governor (currently 1-of-1 — moving it to a real multisig is a near-term commitment), and a treasury. The stack is complete; it simply has no external operators on it yet. Sealed mode adds a one-block commit-reveal flow in which the AI inference runs on 0G Compute (the GLM-5-FP8 model) and the hash of that response is bound into the signed intent and verified on-chain. On quality, the contracts carry 289 passing tests with Slither failing the build on any high-severity finding, and an internal security review surfaced 127 findings before the V4 cutover, with eleven Highs fixed prior to mainnet — though an independent third-party audit has not been done yet, and it is one of the asks below.

That the system actually runs is not a claim but a set of on-chain facts. The first AI-to-policy-to-DEX execution on 0G is transaction `0x7efe51ac…a8a73f` from 24 April 2026, viewable on chainscan.0g.ai. The first sealed-mode execution with a TEE-attestation reveal is transaction `0x0d7334b8…36005e` from 27 April. And there is a live V4 vault at `0xC01523Ef…2bd2`, though it holds only about three dollars of USDC.e and has its fees set to zero, because it is a demonstration vault.

Now the honest status, which we would rather state ourselves than have discovered. We have zero external users and zero external TVL; the one live V4 vault holds roughly three dollars and exists only to prove the pipeline. The make-or-break question — whether the AI actually adds value at low turnover, net of costs — has therefore not yet been tested against a real depositor. We also learned something uncomfortable about the venue: the economics do not close on the current 0G DEX (Jaine). Real pool depths turned out to be several times thinner than we had assumed — roughly five to seven times by current on-chain reads, and they fluctuate — and there is no usable direct USDC-to-BTC or USDC-to-ETH pool, so every such trade must route two hops through the W0G hub, which compounds slippage. The practical consequence is that the largest trade that can execute before the on-chain 3% slippage floor reverts is roughly $800 to $900 for BTC or ETH and about $1,400 for W0G, which caps a functioning vault at roughly the low four figures — on the order of $800 to $1,800 of NAV. This is the concrete reason liquidity is our number-one request. On the positive side, this build cycle included a full money-path and orchestrator audit, after which we fixed — each with a regression test — a high-severity decimals bug that had been bricking every stop-loss sell, a drawdown-halt bypass, a daily-loss gate that was wrongly blocking defensive exits, a fail-open journal state, and an inert loss-streak breaker, alongside dashboard fixes that removed fabricated accuracy and risk numbers; the test suites are green at 289 contract and 199 orchestrator tests. Finally, we are deliberately straight about the trust model: today "verifiable inference" rests on a single ECDSA signing key rather than an on-chain SGX or TDX quote, and we want 0G's help closing exactly that gap.

## 2 · Go-To-Market

Our positioning is honest by design. We sell *verifiable, bounded risk-management* — non-custodial AI trading in which the contract bounds the shape of every trade (position size, slippage, asset whitelist) and operators stake real money — and not "autonomous AI alpha." (To be precise: trade-shape limits are on-chain; drawdown and stop-loss limits are currently enforced off-chain by our orchestrator, and moving them on-chain is on the roadmap.) We intend to earn trust first on capital preservation and provenance, and only later on net yield, once it is proven. The underlying problem is that today's user faces a binary choice between trustless-but-dumb DeFi vaults and smart-but-custodial AI bots; Aegis is the third path, where the AI drives but the contract holds custody and bounds every trade.

Because this is a two-sided marketplace, the cold-start strategy is operator-first: we seed the harder, more valuable side, since a credible operator brings their own audience and demand follows. The sequence runs in four phases. In the first few weeks we run a house anchor vault as Operator #1 on 0G to prove that the full loop — AI proposal, policy enforcement, on-chain execution, honest PnL — runs cleanly over a sustained period; this house vault is explicitly labeled team-operated, capped at no more than half of total TVL, and reported separately so it is never counted as external traction. From roughly weeks three to eight, the main supply push begins: we recruit credible operators who already carry a public, verifiable PnL track record, which lets us skip the ninety-day wait for a cold record and instead bring already-proven strategies into a non-custodial, slashable-stake home. From around week eight onward we open business development with established risk curators and allocators — the desks that write the first $100,000-to-$500,000 institutional checks — because one credible allocation outweighs vanity TVL; this lane requires an ERC-4626 pooled-shares variant, which is on our roadmap. The hardest and highest-quality capital — an anchor depositor or treasury pilot — comes last.

We also hold ourselves to a discipline that matters in a category full of overclaiming. Operator payouts are gated on depositor retention and post-drawdown survival rather than on peak AUM or follower count; traction is reported only as a percentage of *external* TVL; and house vaults are capped and excluded from reputation. This is a deliberate effort to avoid the inflated-track-record trap that has burned other "AI fund" narratives. As for why 0G is the right home: our only published operator manifest and our first on-chain execution proof both live on 0G, which is a genuine provenance head-start. The plan is to prove the core loop end-to-end on 0G first — where the grant and the narrative live — carry that proof forward as a day-one artifact, and treat any move toward deeper liquidity as a separate, data-driven decision rather than a reflexive port. The honest counterweight we are not hiding is that 0G DeFi is thin today, on the order of a few million dollars of TVL, which is exactly why venue and liquidity support sit at the top of our ask.

## 3 · Resources Needed

This is the most concrete section, because it is where 0G can act. First and most important is deeper 0G DEX liquidity — direct USDC-to-BTC and USDC-to-ETH pools and materially deeper W0G-hub pools, or liquidity incentives that achieve the same — because that is what lifts the roughly $1,800 NAV capacity wall and lets the alpha math close on 0G itself, keeping us 0G-native rather than forcing a move purely to find liquidity. As a complementary path that keeps 0G at the core, Aegis is built as a 0G-anchored, multi-chain execution layer: the agent's compute, identity, slashable stake, reputation, and governance stay on 0G, while only *execution* routes to wherever the deepest liquidity is — so if 0G depth cannot reach the needed size in time, we extend execution to deeper-liquidity EVM chains such as Arbitrum (where our infrastructure is already deployed), Base, and others, via the same vault bytecode that is replay-safe by chain-id; this extends 0G's reach rather than leaving it, and deepening 0G liquidity simply keeps more of that execution on 0G. Second, we need introductions on both sides of the marketplace: since we have zero operators today, one or two credible external AI or quant operators to seed supply, and one established risk curator/allocator for a first allocation. Third, we need audit support — a partner slot or funding for a first independent audit covering the V4 vault, the sealed-mode and attestation path, and the planned ERC-4626 pooled variant — which is what unblocks serious capital. Fourth, a milestone-tied grant would fund the path that matters: deploying V4 and running one guarded, low-turnover vault and publishing a real on-chain net-PnL number, while also covering the governance and key-security remediations described below. Fifth, on 0G Compute, we would value reliable model availability for GLM-5-FP8 and compute credits for sustained backtesting and alpha measurement, plus guidance and tooling for on-chain enclave-quote verification so that "verifiable inference" can become a real on-chain attestation rather than just a key. Sixth, on 0G Storage, help productionizing the decision-journal recompute-proof path — currently off by default — which also hardens the durability of our audit trail. And seventh, once a real net-PnL figure exists, co-marketing in the form of a joint case study on our honest differentiator of capital preservation plus slashable stake — gated on a verifiable realized number, never on a promised APY.

## 4 · 0G Deep Incubation or Acceleration

Our answer is yes, and the reason it fits is straightforward: every blocker above — liquidity access, ecosystem business development for operators and allocators, audit, security and compute and storage engineering support, and milestone capital — is exactly what an incubation or acceleration program exists to remove. Because we are already 0G-native, deeper alignment is a natural step rather than a pivot.

Concretely, we would commit to a roughly ninety-day, 0G-first plan: close the two credibility gaps by moving the governor to a real multisig and the signing key to an HSM or remote signer; ship fee collection so that the protocol revenue loop is finally live, since it currently earns zero; seed three to five external operators with team TVL reported separately; achieve the first external V4 execution together with the first real fee collected; and publish a transparent operator-and-depositor unit-economics model alongside the first real on-chain net-PnL from a guarded vault. From the program in return, we would want the resources named in the previous section, a 0G technical sponsor for the Compute, Storage, and TEE items, and warm introductions for operators and allocators. It is equally important to say what we are *not* asking 0G to underwrite: we are not asking for the marketing of unproven yield. We want to prove the number first, together.
