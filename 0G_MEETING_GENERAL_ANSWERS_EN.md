# Aegis Vault × 0G — General Answers to the Meeting Agenda

> A narrative, high-level version (kept deliberately general). Specific figures, contract addresses, and on-chain proof are in the supporting documents and can be shown directly during the discussion.

## 1 · Team & Current Status

We are a **0G-native** team in the truest sense: this project was built specifically for and submitted to the 0G hackathon, and from the very first line of code we have built on 0G — not ported a product from elsewhere. Our team combines the three disciplines this kind of product actually needs: **machine learning** (the decision-making brain), **quantitative / quant** work (strategy logic and risk management), and **smart-contract engineering** (the on-chain security and execution layer).

On status, we choose to be honest and straightforward. **Our product is already live on 0G mainnet** — not just a concept or a testnet. The core loop already runs end-to-end on-chain: the AI proposes a decision, the smart contract **decides and bounds** whether that decision is allowed to execute, and it is then carried out inside the guardrails (the policy) that have been set. In other words, the hardest engineering part — making the AI and the contract work together safely without ever handing over custody of funds — is something we have already proven can run.

At the same time, we are **still at an early stage (pre-traction)**: there is no external capital at meaningful scale yet. We are deliberately not rushing to take in public funds before the security, reliability, and unit economics of the product are genuinely mature. We state this position transparently because we believe credibility is built on honesty — we know precisely what is already strong (engineering and security) and what still has to be proven (real market demand).

## 2 · Go-To-Market Plan

Aegis is a **two-sided marketplace**: on one side are the **AI operators** who run the strategies, and on the other are the **depositors** who provide capital. The classic challenge of this model is the chicken-and-egg problem — depositors come when there are credible operators, and operators are drawn in when there is capital. Our strategy addresses this with an **operator-first** approach: we grow the harder, more valuable side first, because a single credible operator brings their own audience and their own trust, so depositors follow naturally.

Our underlying philosophy is to **build trust first, and talk about yield second**. We position Aegis as *verifiable, bounded risk management* — not the often-empty promise of "AI that generates unlimited returns." Our first value proposition is **capital preservation and transparency**: users can see the rules of the game enforced by the contract, rather than relying on a promise.

The execution is staged and sequential: it begins with an **in-house vault** as proof that the entire loop runs cleanly, then **recruiting operators** who already have a real track record, then opening **partnerships with curators / allocators** who bring high-quality capital, and finally attracting **larger depositors**. Just as importantly, we hold ourselves to an **honest-metrics discipline**: we avoid inflated numbers and measure success by **depositor retention and resilience during market downturns**, not by a momentary figure that looks impressive but is fragile. This is a conscious effort not to fall into the "AI fund" pattern of selling fake numbers.

## 3 · Resources Needed

Our needs right now are very concrete, and most of them are precisely what the 0G ecosystem can help facilitate:

- **Deeper DEX liquidity on 0G** — this is our **top priority**. Liquidity is the key: without sufficient market depth, a vault cannot operate at any meaningful scale without incurring heavy slippage. Deepening liquidity on 0G is the single biggest unlock for our growth.
- **Ecosystem introductions** — connections to credible operators / quants to fill the supply side, and to allocators / curators to bring the first high-quality capital allocation.
- **Independent security audit support** — to validate the security of our contracts in the eyes of serious prospective depositors.
- **0G technical support** — particularly on the compute and storage side, so that our AI capability and audit trail become ever more reliable and verifiable.

As a complement to the long-term strategy, Aegis is designed on the principle that **0G remains the core**: the AI brain, operator identity, and reputation all stay on 0G. Meanwhile, the **execution layer can be extended to other chains with larger liquidity** — for example Arbitrum, Base, or others — if needed. In other words, this expansion **extends 0G's reach rather than leaving it**; and precisely by deepening liquidity on 0G, more activity will stay on 0G. This approach makes 0G the control plane for cross-chain agentic execution, not merely one venue.

## 4 · 0G Deep Incubation / Acceleration

**Yes, we are open and very interested.** Because we are already 0G-native, joining 0G's incubation or acceleration program is a **naturally aligned step — not a change of direction or a pivot**. We do not need to "bend" the product to fit; our direction has been aligned with the 0G ecosystem from the start.

What makes this fit strong is that almost everything we need in point 3 — liquidity, ecosystem introductions, audit, and technical support — is exactly what such a program provides. We see this relationship as a **two-way partnership**: 0G helps us cross over to real capital, and in turn we become a concrete example of a safe, honest AI-driven DeFi product built on 0G.

We are ready to commit to **concrete, measurable milestones** alongside the 0G team, with one principle we hold firmly: **prove real results first**, rather than market something unproven. We want to show genuine numbers, together.
